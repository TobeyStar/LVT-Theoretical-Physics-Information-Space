\section{Power Requirements and Architecture}
The Nilsson-Drive (ND-1) requires extremely high instantaneous power to drive the PFE laser and SRC magnets so that the product value ($C \cdot O$) is forced below the local threshold field ($T_{\mathrm{local}}$). The system is designed around a hybrid architecture: a baseload reactor for continuous operation and an SMES bank for pulse discharge.

\subsection{Power Budget (LVT Thermodynamics)}
According to the metrological standards established in \textit{Document} 4
, the primary thermodynamic energy cost for active isolation is defined as $E_{\mathrm{iso}} = \kappa_N \cdot C \cdot \ln(O^{-1})$. For a standard ND-1 chassis (possessing an enormous informational complexity $C$), this results in the following operational budget:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{PFE-Laser (Peak):} 500 MW (Discharge under $< 100$ ns to choke the final fraction of decoherence and logically liberate the node-pointer).
    \item \textbf{SRC-Magnets (Continuous):} 2.5 MW (Cryogenic cooling and maintenance of the Faraday shield against external network pings).
    \item \textbf{NavCom \& IVM (Variable):} 50-150 kW (Depending on the degree of informational frame-rotation for $\Delta v$-compensation).
    \item \textbf{Hotel Load (Life Support):} 25 kW.
\end{itemize}


\section{Primary Power Source: MSR-Thorium}
To achieve the necessary energy density for macroscopic interstellar operation, the ND-1 utilizes a compact \textbf{Molten Salt Reactor (MSR)} operating on the Thorium cycle.




\subsection{Reactor Specifications}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Type:} LFTR (Liquid Fluoride Thorium Reactor).
    \item \textbf{Fuel:} $^{233}$U dissolved in FLiBe salt ($LiF-BeF_2$).
    \item \textbf{Thermal Power:} 40 MWth.
    \item \textbf{Electrical Power:} 18 MWe (via Brayton-cycle turbines).
    \item \textbf{Weight:} 4.5 tons (including Tungsten/Polymer radiation shielding).
    \item \textbf{Passive Safety:} "Freeze Plug" technology. If cooling fails, the salt solidifies and immediately stops the reaction without the need for external power.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Pulse Storage (SMES)}
The reactor generates the baseload (18 MWe) but cannot deliver the 500 MW required for the PFE discharge. The energy is therefore buffered in a \textbf{Superconducting Magnetic Energy Storage (SMES)}.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Capacity:} 2.5 GJ per toroidal bank.
    \item \textbf{Discharge Time:} $< 100$ nanoseconds (Critical to rewrite the pointer before the matrix's hysteresis $\Gamma$ forces a rendering).
    \item \textbf{Material:} YBCO high-temperature superconductors cooled to 20 K.
    \item \textbf{Charge Time:} Approx. 3 minutes between pointer-recodings (at full reactor power).
\end{itemize}
